📜 The Pashtuns: Understanding History and Culture

Defence and Diplomacy Journal Vol. 12 No. 2 (2023)

Introduction

The Pashtuns are a major ethnic group of South and Central Asia, living mainly in Afghanistan and Pakistan. Their identity is shaped by geography, tribal systems, language, religion, and centuries of political conflict.

Modern Political Context

The fall of Kabul in August 2021 brought renewed geopolitical focus on Pashtun regions. Pakistan expected improved security, but militant dynamics such as TTP activity created new instability.

Geography of Pashtun Land

Pashtunistan spans the region between the Indus River and the Hindu Kush mountains.

Durand Line Division

  • Pashto language
  • Tribal identity
  • Cultural traditions
  • Family-based structure
  • Sunni Islam (mostly Hanafi)

Pashtunwali (Code of Life)

  • Melmastia – Hospitality
  • Nang – Honor
  • Badal – Justice
  • Nanawatai – Forgiveness
  • Jirga – Council system

Conclusion

Pashtun history reflects geography, resistance, and tribal culture shaping South Asia.
