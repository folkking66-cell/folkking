📜 The Pashtuns: Understanding History and Culture

Defence and Diplomacy Journal Vol. 12 No. 2 (2023)

Introduction

The Pashtuns are a major ethnic group of South and Central Asia, living mainly in Afghanistan and Pakistan. Their identity is shaped by geography, tribal systems, language, religion, and centuries of political conflict.

Modern Political Context

The fall of Kabul in August 2021 brought renewed geopolitical focus on Pashtun regions. Pakistan expected improved security, but militant dynamics such as TTP activity created new instability.

Geography of Pashtun Land

Pashtunistan spans the region between the Indus River and the Hindu Kush mountains. It is a mountainous frontier zone historically shaped by migration, trade routes, and invasions.

Durand Line Division

The Pashtuns are divided between Pakistan and Afghanistan by the Durand Line (2,640 km). Despite this, they share:
  • Pashto language
  • Tribal identity
  • Cultural traditions
  • Family-based social structure
  • Sunni Islamic beliefs (mostly Hanafi)

Historical Invasions

The Pashtun region has experienced repeated invasions:
  • Greeks (Alexander the Great)
  • Persians
  • Arabs
  • Mongols
  • Turks
  • Mughals
  • British Empire
  • Soviet Union
  • United States
Despite this, complete long-term control was rarely achieved.

Khyber Pass

The Khyber Pass is one of the most historically important mountain routes in Asia. It has been used for invasions, trade, and cultural exchange for centuries.

Pashtun Identity

Pashtuns share a strong sense of lineage, tribal belonging, and cultural pride. There are multiple theories about their origin including Aryan, Semitic, and mixed ancestry views.

Population

  • Pakistan: ~31 million Pashtuns
  • Afghanistan: ~15 million Pashtuns
Karachi hosts one of the largest Pashtun urban populations in the world.

Pashtunwali (Code of Life)

Pashtunwali is an unwritten tribal ethical system.

Core Principles:

  • Melmastia – Hospitality
  • Nang – Honor
  • Badal – Justice / revenge
  • Nanawatai – Forgiveness / refuge
  • Jirga – Council system
Honor is the central value of Pashtun society.

Culture

Pashtun culture includes poetry, music, storytelling, and dance. Major elements:
  • Pashto poetry (Rahman Baba, Khushal Khan Khattak)
  • Attan dance
  • Rubab music
  • Oral traditions

Modern History

Pashtun regions have been central to:
  • British colonial frontier conflicts
  • Cold War (Soviet invasion of Afghanistan)
  • Post-9/11 war on terror
  • Taliban resurgence
These events shaped modern instability and geopolitics.

Conclusion

Pashtun history reflects a combination of geography, resistance, tribal culture, and political complexity. Understanding their society is essential for understanding South and Central Asian geopolitics.
