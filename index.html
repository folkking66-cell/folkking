📜 Pashtun Civilization (Deep History)

The Pashtun people are one of the oldest and most historically rich ethnic groups in South and Central Asia. Their identity is built on thousands of years of tribal traditions, resistance, and cultural development.

🌍 Origins

Pashtuns are believed to descend from ancient Indo-Iranian tribes who settled in the Hindu Kush region. Over time, they developed a strong tribal system based on honor, independence, and oral tradition.

📜 Historical Era

Pashtun regions have been influenced by major empires including Alexander the Great, the Maurya Empire, Islamic Caliphates, Mongols, Mughals, and the British Empire. Despite invasions, Pashtun tribes preserved independence through mountainous geography and tribal unity.

🏛 Durrani Empire

Founded in 1747 by Ahmad Shah Durrani, this empire became the foundation of modern Afghanistan. At its peak, it controlled large parts of Iran, Pakistan, India, and Central Asia.

🧬 Tribes

Major tribes include Yousafzai, Afridi, Durrani, Khattak, Ghilzai, and many others, each with unique traditions and leadership structures.

🌿 Culture & Traditions

Pashtun culture is based on hospitality, honor, bravery, and strong family systems. Poetry, music, and storytelling are deeply important.

⚖ Pashtunwali Code

Melmastia (hospitality), Nang (honor), Badal (justice), Nanawatai (forgiveness), Jirga (council system).
