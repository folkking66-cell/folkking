Introduction

The Pashtuns are a major ethnic group living mainly in Afghanistan and Pakistan. Their identity is shaped by tribal structure, geography, language, and centuries of history.

Geography

Pashtun regions stretch from the Indus River to the Hindu Kush mountains. This area has historically been a major frontier for trade, migration, and invasions.

Historical Invasions

The region has seen many invasions including:
  • Alexander the Great (Greeks)
  • Persians
  • Arabs
  • Mongols
  • Mughals
  • British Empire
  • Soviet Union
  • United States (modern era)
Despite this, local tribal systems remained strong.

Pashtunwali Code

The traditional code of life:
  • Melmastia – Hospitality
  • Nang – Honor
  • Badal – Justice / revenge
  • Nanawatai – Refuge / forgiveness
  • Jirga – Tribal council system

Culture

Pashtun culture includes poetry, music, storytelling, and dance. Key elements:
  • Pashto poetry (Rahman Baba, Khushal Khan Khattak)
  • Attan traditional dance
  • Rubab musical instrument
  • Oral storytelling traditions

Conclusion

Pashtun history reflects resilience, tribal identity, and continuous interaction with regional and global powers over centuries.
