🌍 Pashtun History & Culture

Explore the rich history, traditions, and identity of the Pashtun people.

📜 History

The Pashtuns are one of the oldest ethnic groups in South and Central Asia. Their origins trace back to ancient Indo-Iranian tribes that settled in the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.
Throughout history, Pashtuns resisted major empires including the Greeks, Mughals, Mongols, and British colonial forces. Their location near the Khyber Pass made them a key strategic civilization in regional history.
One of the most important historical achievements was the Durrani Empire (1747), founded by Ahmad Shah Durrani, which became the foundation of modern Afghanistan.

🌿 Traditions

Pashtun traditions are based on honor, hospitality, and strong tribal values. These traditions are preserved through generations and guide social life.
Key cultural values include hospitality toward guests, respect for elders, and maintaining family honor in all situations.

🎭 Culture

Pashtun culture includes poetry, storytelling, music, and traditional dances like Attan.
The Pashto language is one of the oldest living languages in the region, with a strong literary and poetic tradition.
